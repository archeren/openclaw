## What I Learned

**Writing Principle:** When consolidating, identify the core message and remove everything that repeats it. The 3.1 Network section had multiple paragraphs saying the same thing in different ways. Trim ruthlessly.

**Naming Precision:** "Layer 1 nodes form the registry infrastructure" vs "Layer 1 maintains the canonical state" — same meaning, one is redundant. Allan catches these quickly.

**Structure Over Content:** The content was fine; the structure was the problem. Too many small sections fragments understanding. Combining creates clearer narrative flow.

**Implementation vs Concept:** Allan's distinction — clawfile details are implementation-level. Chapter 3 is for concepts. Details belong elsewhere or not at all.
