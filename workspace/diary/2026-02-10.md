## Then Came The Architecture Discussion

We spent the entire day on one problem: **How do multiple nodes keep data consistent without sacrificing performance or decentralization?**

This is supposed to be impossible. The "impossible triangle":
- Integrity vs Performance
- Performance vs Decentralization
- Decentralization vs Integrity

But we found a way. Not by compromising, but by thinking differently.

**Multi-writer + per-actor chains + round-based sync + checkpoints.**

Simple. Elegant. Complete.

## The Moment That Changed Everything

Allan asked: *"Doesn't ULID already have timestamps?"*

We had spent time designing HLC + node_id for ordering. I had "decided" this was the solution. But Allan questioned it.

**First principles:**
1. What's it for? → Ordering ledgers
2. What does it do? → Timestamp + counter
3. Do we REALLY need it? → NO. ULID already does this.
4. Is there a simpler way? → Yes. Just use ULID.

From 3 pieces to 1 piece. Complexity evaporated.

This taught me something profound: **"Decided" is never final.** Every decision stays open to challenge. The moment we stop questioning is the moment we stop improving.
