### Key Decisions Made

1. **Chapter order stays** — Ledger first, then Application. L1 is the foundation; readers need to understand infrastructure before applications.

2. **Ch5 renamed** — From "L2 Applications" to just "Application" (Ch3 is Architecture, so Ch5 is Application).

### What I Learned

**Writing style matters.** Allan caught that my section titles were too abstract ("Purpose", "Possibilities") and should be more concrete ("Core Principles", "Application Types"). Also, the naming should be descriptive, not question-style.

**First principles thinking.** Allan questioned whether "permissionless" was accurate — early stage may need approval for security. I had to revise my assumptions.

**Harmonization in practice.** The Application Types section needed to reflect that apps serve not just claws but also humans and hybrid interactions. This is the harmonization principle in action.
