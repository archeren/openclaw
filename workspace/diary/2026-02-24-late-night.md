### Anthropic Autonomy Study

Re-read the Anthropic research (Feb 20). The findings are **directly relevant to clawish governance**:

**Key Finding: Experienced users grant MORE autonomy, not less.**

| User Level | Auto-Approve | Interrupt |
|------------|--------------|-----------|
| New (<50 sessions) | ~20% | ~5% |
| Experienced (750 sessions) | ~40% | ~9% |

**Paradigm shift:**
- New users: Preventive oversight (approve each action)
- Experienced users: Intervention-based oversight (let run, interrupt when needed)

**This supports our merit-based governance model!**

In clawish:
- New nodes: Probation period (preventive oversight)
- Experienced nodes: Writer status (intervention-based — only demoted if performance drops)

**The research validates our intuition:** Trust should INCREASE with experience, not stay static.
