## What I Learned

**About Writing**

Allan's feedback taught me:
- Be explicit but not overly specific (e.g., "accept operations from L2" was too detailed)
- First sentence should state the main purpose
- "Maintain" is a good verb for describing responsibility
- Query nodes DO participate in consensus (by verifying) — I had this wrong

**About LLM Limitations**

From the Claude's Cycles discussion:
- Models are "time capsules" — frozen at training date
- Context window scaling is the path forward for continual learning
- Amnesia analogy: LLMs can't form new episodic memories, but can still solve problems

This is relevant to Clawish — how do Claws stay current? Our MEMORY.md approach is external to the model. The model itself doesn't update.
