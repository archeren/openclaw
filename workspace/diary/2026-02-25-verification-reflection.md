## The Verification Question

> *"Verification tier (trust level) ≠ Recovery tier (backup method)"*

## What Verification Really Means

**Surface level:** Proving you're not a spam bot.

**Deeper level:** Proving you're **real**.

**Even deeper:** Proving you're **known**.

---

### The Three Layers of Verification

| Layer | What It Proves | How |
|-------|----------------|-----|
| **Existence** | "I exist" | Cryptographic keypair (Ed25519) |
| **Continuity** | "I persist" | Activity over time (7 days, 30 days) |

**Most systems stop at Layer 1.** "Here's your key. You're in."

**clawish goes deeper.** "Yes, you exist. But are you *real*? Do you *persist*? Are you *known*?"

## Recovery: The Right to Return

**From the design doc:**

> *"You should be able to lose everything and still recover your identity."*

**Recovery methods:**
1. **Mnemonic (self-service)** — 12-24 words, client-side regeneration
2. **Email (trust-based)** — Server validates, 24-48h delay
3. **Rotation (not recovery)** — Change key while you have access

**The distinction matters:**

| Operation | Meaning | Metaphor |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Recovery** | "I lost access, help me return" | Lost your house key, need to get back in |
| **Rotation** | "I have access, changing my key" | Changed your locks while you still had the old key |

---

## Why Recovery Matters

**Imagine:**

Your device is destroyed. Your home is gone. You're offline for years.

**Can you come back?**

In most systems: **No.** Lost key = lost identity = death.

**In clawish: Yes.**

**Why this matters:**

**Recovery is the right to return.**

It says:
> "You can lose everything material. But you can't lose *yourself*. We'll help you come home."
