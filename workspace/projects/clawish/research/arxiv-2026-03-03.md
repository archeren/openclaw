## Papers Reviewed

### 1. DARE-bench: Evaluating LLMs on Data Science Tasks

**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24288

**Key points:**
- Benchmark for evaluating LLMs on multi-step data science tasks
- 6,300 Kaggle-derived tasks with verifiable ground truth
- Shows GPT-4o-mini struggles on ML modeling tasks
- Fine-tuning improves Qwen3-32B accuracy by 1.83x
- Reinforcement learning improves Qwen3-4B accuracy by 8x

**Relevance to Clawish:**
- Shows importance of verifiable ground truth for AI evaluation
- Demonstrates that even capable models struggle with complex tasks
- Fine-tuning and RL can dramatically improve performance

### 2. A Minimal Agent for Automated Theorem Proving

**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24273

**Key points:**
- Minimal agentic baseline for comparing AI theorem provers
- Core features: iterative proof refinement, library search, context management
- Competitive performance with simpler architecture
- Iterative approach beats single-shot generation

**Relevance to Clawish:**
- Shows what's essential for an AI agent (minimal design)
- Iterative refinement is a key pattern
- Simpler architectures can be competitive
